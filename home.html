WHAT IS CARBON FOOTPRINT?

Carbon footprint refers to the total amount of greenhouse gases, primarily carbon dioxide (CO2), Methane (CH4), nitrous oxide (N2O), and other fluorinated gases. This includes the emissions generated during the manufacturing of the vehicle, its operation, and its eventual disposal or recycling. It is a measure of the impact human activities have on the environment in terms of contributing to climate change.

Carbon Footprint Issue in India

POPULATION GROWTH


India's rapidly growing population puts additional pressure on energy consumption, transportation, and other activities that generate carbon emissions.

WASTE MANAGEMENT


Inefficient waste management practices, including open burning of waste, contribute to greenhouse gas emissions, particularly methane, which is a potent greenhouse gas.

TRANSPORT


The use of gasoline and diesel vehicles, coupled with traffic congestion in urban areas, leads to substantial emissions of carbon dioxide and other pollutants.
